# Most enterprises can't answer five basic questions about their own data

- **What data do we have, and where does it live?** — it's spread across many clouds, warehouses, and file stores.
- **What does it mean, and can we trust it?** — definitions conflict and quality is unknown.
- **How sensitive is it, and which regulations apply?** — personal and regulated data is often unlabelled.
- **Who is allowed to use it?** — access is managed inconsistently, person by person.
- **Can we prove all of this to an auditor?** — evidence is reconstructed by hand under deadline.

Today these answers live in spreadsheets, tribal knowledge, and email — not in a system.

# Know exactly what data you have

**Data Catalog & Metadata Management**

- A single, searchable inventory of every database, table, file, and cloud-storage location across all connected systems — built and kept current automatically.
- Every asset carries business and technical context: description, owner, steward, business area, sensitivity labels, and linked business terms.
- Trust signals on every asset: data-quality score, certification status, freshness, and business-criticality tier.
- Detail down to each column: data type, keys, sensitivity flags, and automatically-computed statistics.

---

# See where every number comes from

**Data Lineage**

- End-to-end lineage from the original source, through every transformation, to the reports and models that consume the data.
- Trace a single column as it moves and changes across systems.
- Answer instantly the two questions that stall most teams: *where did this number come from,* and *what breaks if we change it?*

---

# One shared language for the business

**Business Glossary**

- Define each business term once — for example "active customer" or "net revenue" — so every team uses the same meaning.
- Link each term to the actual tables and columns that hold the data behind it.
- Clear authority: every definition is marked draft, approved, or retired, so people know which to trust.

---

# Find and protect sensitive data

**Data Classification & Tagging**

- Built-in sensitivity classifications for the most regulated data: PII (personal data), PHI (health data), and PCI-DSS (payment-card data).
- Labels organized into categories and tracked for how widely they are used.
- Classification drives access policy and compliance checks automatically — sensitive data is governed by design.
- Labels already present in source systems are reconciled to governed labels, with conflicts flagged for review.

---

# Make trust measurable

**Data Quality**

- Tests across the six standard dimensions of data health: completeness, accuracy, consistency, timeliness, validity, and uniqueness.
- A quality score on every asset, shown right in the catalog.
- Failed checks raise tracked incidents — open, in review, resolved, dismissed — each with a severity and a written root cause.
- Checks run automatically on a schedule or on demand.

---

# Trust, made official

**Certification**

- Certify an asset as reviewed and dependable; its status and quality score follow it everywhere it appears.
- A consistent review checklist before certification: documented, sensitive data labelled, lineage verified, quality passing, and an owner assigned.
- Certified, high-quality data is surfaced where people search and consume — so they pick the right source with confidence.

---

# Govern close to the business

**Data Domains & Data Products**

- Organize data by business area (domain), each with its own owners and stewards — federated governance that scales without a central bottleneck.
- Package trusted data into curated data products with defined inputs and outputs.
- Ownership attaches to teams, so accountability survives staff and role changes.

---

# Least-privilege access, simply governed

**Access Control & Roles**

- Four clear, standard roles — Admin, Connection Admin, Steward, Viewer — plus custom roles, instead of permissions managed person by person.
- Precise, operation-level permissions on each type of data.
- Domain-scoped access: a team governs only the data in its own business area.
- Conditional access — by ownership, team, domain, role, sensitivity label, or certification status.

---

# Continuous, evidence-backed compliance

**Policy Management**

- Author governance policies — privacy, lifecycle, security, and more — and evaluate the entire estate against them automatically and continuously.
- A live compliance score and violation count for every policy, so gaps are visible at a glance.
- A built-in regulation register linking real obligations to the policies that satisfy them.
- A risk register, with each governance risk linked to a policy, owner, mitigation, and status.

# Prove it — to any auditor

**Audit & Activity History**

- A tamper-evident record of every governed change: the time, exactly what changed (with before-and-after values), and who made it.
- Captured across data assets, business areas, data products, and policies.
- Exportable for reporting and audit review.

# From source to trusted, governed data

**The governance lifecycle**

**Connect → Catalog → Classify → Govern → Comply → Audit**

A continuous loop: data is connected and catalogued, classified and organized, governed by policy and access rules, checked for compliance, and every step is recorded for audit.
